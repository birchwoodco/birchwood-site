We are creative partners who believe the most important work an organization can do is understand who it actually is and learn to communicate that with integrity. We ask questions most strategists won't, sit with complexity most processes skip, and help you find what's real before we tell you how to say it. Most communications work starts outside and moves in. We work the other direction. Identity first. Story second. Expression third.

We sit with organizations at moments of tension, transition, and transformation. Not to hand them a framework, but to do the slow work of listening that makes the right framework possible. The story is always already there. The work is drawing it out.

We are especially at home with faith-based organizations, global initiatives, and mission-driven work — contexts where story carries weight that goes beyond marketing.

The Problem

Here's where the story breaks down.

Plenty of agencies will grow your impressions. They'll build a website, run a campaign, produce a video. But if the story underneath doesn't reflect the actual depth and uniqueness of your organization, none of it sticks. You end up with better packaging around a message that was never properly found. We start underneath.

Brand Misalignment

Your external messaging no longer reflects who you actually are. The brand feels off. Your team can feel it and so can the people you're trying to reach. No amount of new design will close that gap if the story underneath is not solid.

Story Drift

Growth, transition, or change has quietly, unintentionally separated your narrative from your actual mission. Somewhere along the way you lost the thread. You keep producing content, but it doesn't land the way it used to.

Communication Breakdown

If you ask five different people in your organization what you do and get five different answers, you have a story problem. That problem is showing up in every meeting, every pitch, every piece of content you produce.

Threshold Moment

In stories, thresholds are transformation moments. A new leader. A pivot. An expanded mission. Moments of transition are also when identity is most at risk of getting swept up in newness until you lose sight of your core.

How We Work

Every engagement follows three movements, but where you enter depends on what you need. The sequence doesn't change.

01

Identity

Who you actually are.

Most organizations have never had a room where someone asked the real questions. Through facilitated sessions with your stakeholders, we surface the foundation your organization is actually built on. When it's done, your team is aligned and can speak from that foundation with confidence.

  • Story Gap Diagnostic
  • Story Alignment Canvas sessions
  • Proof Stories truth test
  • Story Gap Report
02

Story

How you say it.

Once we know what's real and authentic to your work, we build the language that carries it. We craft a story framework your team can speak from without us in the room. Messaging that holds together across every channel, audience, and context because it all originates from the same true place.

  • Strategic messaging framework
  • Voice and tone guidelines
  • Key messages for consistent communication
  • Team Talking Points
03

Expression

What you put into the world.

We produce films, campaigns, and content built on what we've uncovered together. The difference between our production work and anyone else's is that ours originates from what's distinctly yours — not a brief, not a brand deck, but the real thing. Birchwood and its network of specialists — documentary filmmakers, content strategists, writers, and creatives, some of whom come from within the communities we serve — translate your story into the content your organization needs to reach your audience.

  • Films and documentary video
  • Content strategy and creation
  • Ongoing partnership and retainer
  • Trusted partners for visual identity and brand design
